The foundation is exceptional. Here's where we build on it.

Both venues have earned the kind of Google reputation most operators spend years trying to manufacture — specific, warmly written, overwhelmingly five-star. That reputation is the strongest asset in the funnel. The opportunity is in making sure the marketing puts it front and center at every stage.

  • Top of funnel has headroom. Chateau 1800's 10.5K Instagram followers engage at 0.27% — there's a significant gap between the audience size and the content engaging them. The raw material is already there.
  • Middle of funnel has quick wins. Both venues have strong Google review scores, and a few quick fixes on the inquiry path can meaningfully improve conversion.
  • Bottom of funnel has room to optimize. Both venues are on The Knot and WeddingWire — the opportunity now is fully building out those profiles to outcompete listings that are more complete.

The next four pages go stage by stage and show exactly what moving each lever looks like.

Key Insights

Three themes shaping the strategy.

▲

Reputation > Reach

384 combined Google reviews at 4.85★ is doing more work than any channel should be asked to do alone. The venues are winning on proof — and starving on awareness.

◆

Two Audiences, One Voice Missing

Chateau 1800 targets full-service estate weddings in Effingham/Savannah. Chapel by the Sea targets beachside destination weddings. The content strategy can't treat them as one brand — but the right strategy serves both.

●

Bottom of the Funnel Is Leaking

Chapel by the Sea's 24-hour response time is the clearest quick win — Chateau already meets the 4-hour industry best. Both venues are on The Knot; the opportunity now is optimization, not claiming.
